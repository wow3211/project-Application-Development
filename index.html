✦ UNESCO World Heritage Site

Diriyah

Diriyah is the ancestral heart of the nation, serving as the capital of the First Saudi State. Home to the UNESCO-listed At-Turaif district, it stands as a testament to the Najdi architectural style — a marvel of mud-brick craftsmanship that has endured for centuries.

🎟 Ticket Information

  • General Entry: Free on weekdays until 4 PM
  • Diriyah Pass: 100 SAR
  • Redeemable on food & beverage

📍 Visitor Tips

  • Best time: October – March
  • Guided tours available on-site
  • Evening light shows on weekends

✦ Ancient Nabataean Capital

AlUla

AlUla is a living museum of human civilisation, famous for the monumental tombs of Hegra carved directly into sandstone cliffs by the Nabataeans. It acts as a window into deep antiquity — where ancient rock art, oasis farms, and dramatic landscapes converge in one extraordinary destination.

🎟 Ticket Information

  • Standard Tour: 95 SAR per person
  • Old Town: Free entry
  • Reservation required for Old Town

📍 Visitor Tips

  • Best time: November – April
  • Hegra tours: advance booking needed
  • Hot air balloon rides available
